## 1. Packaging Principle

V2 should sell workflows, not raw technical features.

The user should understand each offer in terms of what job it does:

- understand my claim
- research similar decisions
- find the governing rule
- build a stronger strategy
- analyze a denial
- accelerate professional research

## 2. Tier Model

The official pricing ladder is:

- Free
- Pro
- Professional

Do not introduce more tiers until this model is clear and live.

## 3. Free Tier

### Positioning

Free gives users access to the publication and entry-point research utilities.

This tier should build trust, recurring usage, and newsletter conversion.

### Intended audience

- veterans
- family members helping with a claim
- first-time visitors
- casual researchers
- top-of-funnel advocates

### Included products

#### Publication

- all articles
- all guides
- all news / policy updates
- newsletter signup

#### Research utilities

- BVA Decision Search
- 38 CFR Navigator
- KnowVA / M21-1 Search
- VA Math Calculator
- CAVC Precedent Library

#### Entry-level claim tool

- Nexus Scout Lite

### What "Lite" means

Nexus Scout Lite can include:

- primary-to-secondary condition exploration
- downloadable public evidence packs
- limited guided research prompts

It should not include deep claim-specific analysis or persistent workspace features.

### Free tier purpose

Free is the trust and acquisition layer, not the main monetization layer.

## 4. Pro Tier

### Positioning

Pro is for serious self-directed claim research and strategy building.

This tier is for users who want deeper outputs, more guided workflows, and more repeated usage.

### Intended audience

- veterans actively working a claim
- veterans preparing supplemental claims or HLRs
- power users who need more than search
- some advocates working solo

### Included products

- Claim Research Assistant
- Nexus Scout Pro
- CAVC Case Analyzer
- deeper guided citation-backed research outputs
- saved research sessions
- longer and richer synthesis outputs
- exportable research packets

### Product definitions

#### Claim Research Assistant

Cross-source claim research workflow grounded in BVA, CFR, KnowVA, and CAVC materials.

Primary value:

- faster issue research
- better evidence framing
- stronger claim strategy direction

#### Nexus Scout Pro

Expanded version of Nexus Scout with:

- stronger evidence mapping
- richer secondary-claim reasoning
- claim-building suggestions
- deeper supporting research paths

#### CAVC Case Analyzer

Structured breakdowns of CAVC decisions with:

- holding
- reasoning
- practical significance
- claims-strategy implications

### Pro boundary rules

Pro can include:

- individual research acceleration
- stronger summaries
- deeper issue analysis
- citation-backed outputs

Pro should not include:

- advanced denial deconstruction as a flagship workflow
- attorney-oriented matter workflows
- highest-usage professional capacity

## 5. Professional Tier

### Positioning

Professional is for advocates, accredited agents, and attorneys who need deeper document analysis and more robust legal research workflows.

### Intended audience

- accredited representatives
- VSOs / advocates with heavier usage
- attorneys
- appellate practitioners

### Included products

- Decision Deconstructor
- Appeal Strategy Workbench
- Attorney Research Mode
- advanced CAVC deep-dive workflows
- higher usage limits
- professional-grade export/report outputs

### Product definitions

#### Decision Deconstructor

The flagship proprietary workflow.

Input:

- a VA decision or denial document

Output:

- favorable findings
- denial basis
- issue-by-issue breakdown
- missing evidence
- likely next filing lane
- related legal and case support

#### Appeal Strategy Workbench

A deeper workflow for:

- issue mapping
- appeal-oriented research
- evidence gap review
- structured next-step strategy

#### Attorney Research Mode

A professional research layer optimized for:

- faster appellate research
- more complex issue sets
- denser source handling
- higher-confidence legal workflow support

### Professional boundary rules

Professional is where the heaviest-value and highest-trust workflows should live.

This is the tier for:

- denial analysis
- appeal strategy
- professional research acceleration
- highest-usage product access

## 9. Upgrade Logic

### Free -> Pro

Upgrade message:

Move from search and reading into guided claim strategy.

Use when the user wants:

- deeper issue analysis
- stronger synthesis
- saved work
- research exports
- repeated workflow use

### Pro -> Professional

Upgrade message:

Move from self-directed claim research into professional-grade document analysis and appeal workflows.

Use when the user wants:

- denial analysis
- issue-by-issue breakdowns
- appeal workflows
- heavier usage
- more professional reporting

## 10. Catalog Rules

### Free rules

Free should maximize:

- trust
- SEO
- discoverability
- newsletter conversion

Free should not become so constrained that the product feels unusable.

### Pro rules

Pro should be the default paid tier for serious veterans and solo power users.

Pro should feel clearly stronger than Free, but not so legal-professional that it confuses the market.

### Professional rules

Professional should feel like a true category step-up, not just "Pro with more credits."

The most defensible wedge products should live here first.

## 13. Final Rule

When a tool is added or renamed, decide first:

- Is this Free, Pro, or Professional?
- What user job does it solve?
- Is it a search utility, a strategy workflow, or a document-analysis workflow?

If that answer is unclear, the product is not packaged clearly enough yet.
